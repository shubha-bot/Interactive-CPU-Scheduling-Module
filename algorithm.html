CPU Scheduling Algorithms

First-Come, First-Served (FCFS)

FCFS is the simplest CPU scheduling algorithm. Processes are executed in the order they arrive in the ready queue. It's non-preemptive, meaning once a process starts, it runs until completion.

Pros: Simple to implement, fair in terms of arrival order.

Cons: Can lead to the convoy effect where short processes behind a long process experience long waiting times.

Shortest Job First (SJF)

SJF schedules the process with the smallest burst time next. It's non-preemptive, meaning once a process starts, it runs until completion.

Pros: Minimizes average waiting time and turnaround time.

Cons: May lead to starvation of longer processes.

Shortest Remaining Time First (SRTF)

SRTF is the preemptive version of SJF. The CPU is given to the process with the shortest remaining burst time at every scheduling decision point.

Pros: Can significantly reduce average waiting time.

Cons: Higher complexity and risk of starvation.
